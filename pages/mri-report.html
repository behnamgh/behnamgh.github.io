MRI Report – Left Shoulder

Patient Information

  • Name: Abdolghafari, Behnam
  • Date of Birth: 07.09.1989
  • Examination Date: 15.09.2025
  • Referring Doctor: Dr. Cora Walter, Specialist in Trauma Surgery & Orthopedics
  • Clinic: MVZ BeRaNuk GmbH, Berlin

Clinical Background

Status post left shoulder dislocation on 12.09.2025.

Clinical Question

Rotator cuff, bony injury?

Technique

Ambition MR5300, 1.5 T, MSK coil Gr.M. T1- and T2-weighted images in sagittal, axial, and coronal planes with fat-suppressed sequences.

Findings

  • Normal alignment of the depicted bony structures in coronal images.
  • Axial images show discrete dorsal displacement of the humeral head relative to the glenoid.
  • Marked joint effusion extending into the subcoracoidal recess.
  • Subtle fiber changes of the supraspinatus tendon and infraspinatus tendon without clear rupture.
  • Subscapularis tendon appears intact.
  • Long biceps tendon is positioned correctly in the sulcus.
  • Axial images show an impression of the humeral head (Hill-Sachs lesion) with a posterior superior diameter of ~1 cm.
  • No evidence of a fresh bony injury to the glenoid.
  • Continuity of the middle glenohumeral ligament preserved.
  • No dislocation of the long biceps tendon.
  • Unremarkable appearance of the subacromial space and the deltoid muscle.

Assessment

  • Status post anterior shoulder dislocation with Hill-Sachs defect.
  • No evidence of a Bankart lesion or detachment of the anterior cartilage-labrum complex.
  • Rupture of the middle glenohumeral ligament and anterior capsule structures.
  • Rupture of the subscapularis muscle.
  • No evidence of dislocation of the long biceps tendon.
  • Marked joint effusion.
